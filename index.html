Fractions
Life runs in fractions of time — mini stories cut up into sections. Like a stopwatch counting down a trip to the beach, we count down the in-between before the moment has arrived. We balance time upon our fingers and wait for the seconds, minutes, or hours to keep growing taller or topple over. We wish them to move faster, but when a moment topples too fast, moving ahead with abandon, we wish to slow the stopwatch. Let's ask for our time back — maybe just a last smile, replayed. Or the bad joke your father always tells. You want to roll your eyes in jest again; hear the mock groaning to the end of his joke, even though the end is always the same. You want to hear it again anyway. Just one last time, one fraction more.
